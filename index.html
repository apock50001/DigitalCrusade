The Portfolio

Our documented case against the unchecked advance of artificial intelligence.

Religious & Ethical Concerns

Human Dignity Must Not Be Reduced to Data

Every human being possesses an irreducible dignity that cannot be captured, compressed, or replicated by any statistical model. When artificial intelligence systems are trained on human behaviour and output — our writing, our art, our medical records — they implicitly treat human experience as raw material. This reduction of the sacred to the quantifiable is not progress; it is a profound category error with lasting moral consequences.

AI Should Never Replace Human Moral Judgment

Moral decision-making is not an optimisation problem. It requires conscience, context, lived experience, and genuine accountability. Systems designed to simulate ethical reasoning cannot bear moral responsibility — yet they are increasingly deployed in criminal sentencing, medical triage, and financial decisions that shape human lives. Delegating these judgments to machines represents a dangerous abdication of human responsibility.

  • Algorithmic sentencing removes judicial discretion and humanity
  • Automated hiring denies applicants the right to be seen as full persons
  • AI medical advice removes the irreplaceable dimension of care

Manipulation, Misinformation, and the Erosion of Truth

The industrial-scale generation of synthetic content — convincing fabrications indistinguishable from genuine human expression — poses an existential threat to public discourse. AI-generated disinformation is not merely a technical problem; it is an assault on the conditions necessary for a functioning democracy and shared reality.

Power Concentration Among Technology Corporations

A handful of corporations now control the infrastructure through which AI systems are built, deployed, and monetised. This concentration of power over transformative technology — in private, largely unaccountable hands — is antithetical to human flourishing and democratic governance. The Catholic Church, among many other voices, has called for AI to be developed for the common good, not private profit.

"Technology should serve humanity, not replace it." — Digital Crusade Manifesto, 2024
Why AI Should Not Be Used in Education

The Destruction of Critical Thinking

Education is not merely the transfer of information. It is the slow, difficult cultivation of the capacity to think — to analyse, question, synthesise, and judge. When AI systems provide instant answers, they short-circuit the very process that builds intellectual capability. Students who consult a language model for every essay prompt, every research question, and every mathematical challenge are being deprived of the cognitive struggle through which genuine understanding develops.

  • Reduces engagement with difficult problems
  • Encourages surface-level rather than deep learning
  • Conditions students to accept generated answers uncritically
  • Undermines the habit of independent investigation

Academic Dishonesty at Industrial Scale

AI writing tools have made plagiarism effortless and undetectable. The integrity that academic institutions have built over centuries is being systematically dismantled. When a student submits AI-generated work as their own, they are not merely cheating — they are forfeiting the intellectual development that education is meant to provide.

The Atrophy of Writing as a Human Skill

Writing is thinking made visible. The struggle to articulate a complex idea in clear prose is not incidental to education — it is central to it. As AI writing assistants become ubiquitous, students lose both the incentive and the practice to develop this foundational skill. The long-term consequence is a generation less able to express, argue, and persuade.

Long-Term Educational Consequences

  • Diminished problem-solving capacity across all disciplines
  • Shorter attention spans and reduced tolerance for difficulty
  • Declining intellectual curiosity and intrinsic motivation
  • Weaker interpersonal communication and oral reasoning
  • Generations less capable of sustained, independent thought
Economic Damage from AI Expansion

Automation and the Displacement of Workers

Artificial intelligence does not merely assist workers — it replaces them. From customer service representatives to graphic designers, from paralegal researchers to radiologists, entire occupational categories are being made redundant faster than economies can create alternative employment. Goldman Sachs estimates that 300 million jobs globally are exposed to AI automation. The human costs of this displacement — financial insecurity, loss of purpose, community decline — are being treated as acceptable externalities in the pursuit of efficiency.

  • Entry-level creative and knowledge-work positions disappearing first
  • Middle-skill workers face wage suppression from AI competition
  • Transition support and retraining absent from most AI deployment plans

Unprecedented Wealth Concentration

The productivity gains from artificial intelligence are not being distributed broadly. They are accruing overwhelmingly to the owners of AI systems and the corporations that deploy them. This dynamic — labour replaced by capital, with returns captured by capital — represents a structural acceleration of inequality. Small businesses, independent creators, and working people are subsidising, through their data and their displaced labour, the enrichment of a small technological elite.

The Devaluation of Human Creativity

When AI systems can generate plausible music, images, and text at near-zero marginal cost, the market for human creative work is fundamentally disrupted. This is not merely an economic issue — it strikes at the meaning and value attributed to human creative endeavour. A culture that can manufacture art without artists, stories without storytellers, and music without musicians is a culture that has confused production with creation.

Environmental Damage from AI Infrastructure

The Hidden Energy Cost of Every AI Query

A single query to a large language model consumes approximately ten times more energy than a standard web search. As AI tools are integrated into every digital interaction — search, communication, productivity software, customer service — the aggregate energy demand is staggering. Data centres supporting AI workloads are projected to account for a growing fraction of global electricity consumption, straining grids and driving demand for fossil fuels in regions with insufficient renewable capacity.

  • Training a single large AI model emits hundreds of tonnes of CO₂
  • AI inference (ongoing use) multiplies this by millions of daily queries
  • Grid demand from AI is reversing progress on decarbonisation in some regions

Water: The Invisible Resource Crisis

AI data centres require enormous quantities of water for cooling. Microsoft's own disclosures indicate its global water consumption increased by 34% in a single year, driven largely by AI infrastructure. In water-stressed regions, the siting of data centres creates direct competition with agricultural and residential water needs — a competition in which corporations almost always prevail.

Electronic Waste and Rare Earth Extraction

The hardware demands of AI — specialised graphics processors, custom silicon, and rapidly cycling server infrastructure — are driving an acceleration of electronic waste. The rare earth materials required for this hardware are extracted under conditions that cause severe environmental degradation and, in many cases, serious human rights violations. The environmental cost of AI is not confined to electricity bills; it is written into the landscapes and communities where its physical substrate is sourced.

  • GPU replacement cycles far shorter than conventional IT hardware
  • Rare earth mining causes irreversible ecological damage
  • E-waste from AI hardware is projected to grow faster than recycling capacity

How We Fight Back

Organised, principled, and relentless — our resistance takes many forms.

Public Education

Workshops & Lectures

  • Community workshops on AI literacy
  • School presentations for students aged 10–18
  • Public lectures at libraries and civic centres
  • Free downloadable educational materials
  • Teacher training programmes

Digital Literacy

  • How AI systems actually work — demystified
  • Recognising and resisting AI-generated misinformation
  • Understanding and protecting your privacy
  • Why human-created work has irreplaceable value
  • Tools for critical evaluation of AI outputs
Political Action

Legislative Advocacy

  • Transparency and disclosure requirements for AI systems
  • Stronger regulation of automated decision-making
  • Mandatory human oversight in high-stakes domains
  • Restrictions on monopolistic data accumulation
  • International coordination on AI governance

Economic Measures

  • Fair taxation of large AI corporations
  • Levies on AI-generated content at scale
  • Compensation frameworks for data and creative work used to train AI
  • Investment in human education and vocational training
  • Support for independent creators and small businesses
Awareness Campaigns

Public Demonstrations

  • Organised marches and rallies in major cities
  • Vigils outside AI company headquarters
  • Symbolic protests at tech conferences
  • International day of action coordination

Media & Communications

  • Op-eds and investigative journalism partnerships
  • Documentary filmmaking and distribution
  • Social media campaigns with original human-made content
  • Podcast series on the human cost of AI

News & Campaigns

Reports from the front lines of the resistance.

Campaign Report

Mass Walkout at European AI Summit Draws International Coverage

12 November 2024  |  Brussels, Belgium

Over two thousand activists, academics, and workers staged a coordinated walkout during the opening session of the European AI Governance Summit, carrying banners reading "Human Judgment Cannot Be Automated" and "Our Data, Our Lives." The action drew live coverage from fifteen major broadcasters and forced an emergency session on labour protections into the conference agenda.

Investigation

Exclusive: Major AI Laboratory Consuming Water at Rate of 50,000 Households

4 November 2024  |  Digital Crusade Investigations

A Digital Crusade investigation, drawing on utility filings and satellite imagery, has revealed that a leading AI research facility in the American Southwest is consuming water at a rate equivalent to the daily usage of fifty thousand households. The facility sits in a region classified as facing extreme drought risk. Local agricultural communities report pressure on water allocations they depend upon for their livelihoods.

Education

Schools Outreach Programme Reaches 40,000 Students Across Eight Countries

28 October 2024  |  Global

Our volunteer-led schools programme has now reached forty thousand secondary school students with its curriculum on critical thinking, digital literacy, and the risks of AI dependency in education. Participating schools report measurable increases in student confidence in independent research and a marked decline in AI tool use for assessed work.

Policy

Digital Crusade Brief Cited in European Parliament AI Act Debate

19 October 2024  |  Strasbourg

Our policy brief on automated decision-making and human dignity was formally cited in three separate contributions to the European Parliament debate on amendments to the AI Act. MEPs from four political groups referenced our data on employment displacement and called for stricter requirements on human oversight in consequential AI systems.

Community

Community Forums in Twelve Cities Discuss AI's Impact on Local Economies

7 October 2024  |  Multiple Locations

Simultaneous community discussion forums held across twelve cities on three continents brought together workers, small business owners, artists, and educators to share experiences of AI's economic impact. The forums produced a joint declaration calling on local governments to establish human employment protection charters and to resist incentives offered by AI companies for favourable regulatory treatment.
